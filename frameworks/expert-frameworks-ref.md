# Expert Frameworks — Lean Reference

> Claude already knows these frameworks in depth. This file maps each framework
> to the specific audit question and what to look for during a teardown.
> Full reference: `frameworks/expert-frameworks.md`

| # | Framework | Key Audit Question | What to Look For |
|---|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **HEART** (Google) | Does this screen help or hurt Happiness, Engagement, Adoption, Retention, Task Success? | Weakest HEART dimension = priority fix |
| 2 | **Hook Model** (Nir Eyal) | Is the habit loop complete: Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment? | Missing/weak phase breaks the loop. Most apps fail at Variable Reward or Investment |
| 3 | **Nielsen's 10 Heuristics** | Does this screen violate any of the 10 usability heuristics? | Visibility, real-world match, user control, consistency, error prevention, recognition > recall, flexibility, minimalism, error recovery, help |
| 4 | **JTBD** (Christensen) | Does the app serve the actual job the user hired it for? (Functional + Emotional + Social) | Map the 8-step Job Map: Define → Locate → Prepare → Confirm → Execute → Monitor → Modify → Conclude. Most fail at Locate, Monitor, Conclude |
| 5 | **Fogg MAP** | B = Motivation × Ability × Prompt — are all three present at the action moment? | Simplicity factors: Time, Money, Physical effort, Brain cycles, Social deviance, Non-routine |
| 6 | **Reforge Growth** | Which growth loops exist? What's the retention type coverage? Is monetization-engagement aligned? | Viral / Content / Paid / Product loops. New / Current / Resurrected retention. Engagement-first vs. monetization-first |
| 7 | **Norman's Design Principles** | Are affordances, signifiers, mapping, feedback, conceptual model, and constraints correct? | Root cause of "confusing UI" or "users don't know what to do" |
| 8 | **Kano Model** | Is this feature Must-Be, One-Dimensional, Attractive, Indifferent, or Reverse? | Missing Must-Be = critical. Prioritise One-Dimensional. Recommend Attractive. Kill Reverse |
| 9 | **AARRR Pirate Metrics** | Which lifecycle stage does this screen/flow serve? Is every stage covered? | Acquisition → Activation → Retention → Revenue → Referral |
| 10 | **Cagan Product Discovery** | Does this feature pass Value, Usability, Feasibility, Viability risk checks? | Value Risk and Usability Risk failures → redesign before optimising |

## India Consumer Adjustments (Quick Reference)

- **Fogg Ability**: Money sensitivity 3-5× higher. Brain cycles matter more (lower app literacy Tier 2-3). Non-routine critical (first-gen smartphone users).
- **Hook Triggers**: WhatsApp is primary external trigger (not email/SMS). Internal triggers are community/family-driven.
- **AARRR Referral**: WhatsApp-native share mechanics required. Certificate sharing to Instagram secondary.
- **Kano Must-Be**: Hindi/regional language, low data/offline mode, UPI/wallet payment.
- **Pricing**: Annual beats monthly. ₹1 trial = "scam" perception in some segments. Free-to-paid needs 5-10× more value demo than US.
